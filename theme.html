New Zealand Tourist Destinations

Cape Reinga

Located at the top most part of New Zealand, you can see the two oceans meet, the Tasman Sea and the Pacific Ocean. It features an iconic white lighthouse. According to Māori legend, this is where the spirits of the dead depart into the ocean and return to Hawaiki.



Cathedral Cove

Accessible by walk or on boat, this beautiful landscape has been carved over thousands of years. The beach has white sands shaded by pohutukawa trees. A perfect secluded place for a picnic.



Wai-O-Tapu

The Māori translation for “sacred waters”. A showcase of geothermal activity. Known for its hot springs, boiling mud pools and geysers.



Tongariro National Park

Has three active volcanic mountains, Mount Ruapehu, Mount Tongariro and Mount Ngauruhoe. World class hikes. The famous Tongariro Alpine Crossing is a 19km hike over Mt. Tongariro.



Hokitika Gorge

Walk across turquoise waters surrounded by native rimu and podocarp forest.



Lake Tekapo

One of the perfect spots for stargazing. Home to an iconic church, the Good Shepherd.



Wanaka

A popular ski destination that doubles as a great summer get-away town. Home to the most photographed tree in New Zealand titled 'That Wanaka Tree' a willow tree that sits alone on Lake Wānaka.



Queenstown

A very popular tourist destination with a plethora of outdoor activities, from skiing, bungy jumping, skydiving, jet boating and more. Located alongside Lake Wakatipu, there's stunning views to wake up to every day you're there.


New Zealand is home to many beautiful views, this is only a glimpse of what it has to offer.
